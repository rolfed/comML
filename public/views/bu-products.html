Walnut

Walnut is one of the most versatile and widespread hardwoods, its strength and durability, along with its variety of shades and grains, validating its excellent woodworking properties.

Ash

Ash is a tough, heavy and permeable hardwood. Its grain is similar to oak and is white to light brown in color Ash is widely used for structural frames and furniture, and because it is often less expensive than other hardwoods, ash is widely sought after.

Aspen

Aspen has low bending classification and has medium shock resistance. Its wood is light and fairly soft.

Bass

Bass Wood is easy to work, being soft and light and although it finishes well, it has poor nail holding characteristics. It is usually white to light brown in color and any defects in the wood are rather uncommon.

Teak

Teak is a heavy, strong and durable hardwood similar in its woodworking properties to walnut. It is a yellow to dark brown wood often used in furniture making.

Beech

Beech, similar to walnut and teak in its woodworking properties, is a hard, strong, relatively nonporous and heavy hardwood and is similar to appearance to maple. Beech is often used for frames and veneers. Birch is a hard, heavy and close grained hardwood with a light or reddish color.
